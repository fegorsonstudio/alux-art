# Alux Art — Brand Guide

## 1. Brand Identity

### Full Name
**Alux Art and Frames**

### Short Name / Display Name
**Alux Art**

### Core Proposition
Alux Art is an AI photoshoot studio that turns your selfies and casual photos into a full set of professional editorial images — without prompts, without a photographer, without booking.

Upload photos. Get 10 professional images. Done.

### Positioning Statement
Alux Art occupies the intersection of accessible luxury and autonomous creative technology. It is not a filter app. It is not a photo editor. It is a studio — one that uses AI to do what a photographer, a stylist, and a retoucher would do together, at a fraction of the cost and time, available to anyone in Nigeria and globally.

### Category
AI-Powered Autonomous Photography Studio

---

## 2. Mission and Vision

### Mission
To give every person the power to produce editorial-quality photographs of themselves — on demand, without technical knowledge, without a studio appointment, and without compromise on identity fidelity.

### Vision
A world where professional imagery is not reserved for brands, celebrities, or those who can afford a commercial shoot. Alux Art is the studio in your pocket.

### Strategic Promise
Three things Alux Art never trades away:
1. **Identity fidelity** — every generated image preserves your exact face, skin tone, body build, and likeness. Not someone who looks like you. You.
2. **Editorial quality** — the output is styled, lit, and composed to professional standard.
3. **Zero prompts** — the intelligence is in the product. The user's job is to upload photos.

## 3. Target Audience

### Primary — The Nigerian Creative Professional
- Age 22–38
- Lagos, Abuja, Port Harcourt — urban, social-media-literate
- Creator, entrepreneur, content strategist, fashion-forward professional
- Spends on appearance; understands the value of a professional image for LinkedIn, Instagram, brand decks
- Has a smartphone, often has Google Workspace, familiar with Paystack checkout
- Pain: professional photoshoots are expensive (₦50,000–₦200,000+ for a decent one), require scheduling, depend on photographer availability
- Alux Art solves this: ₦15,000 for 10 editorial images, delivered in minutes

### Secondary — Global Creator / Remote Professional
- Diasporan Africans, digital nomads, solopreneurs
- Pays in USD
- Needs a consistent visual identity across platforms
- Values autonomy and speed

### Anti-audience
- Users who want photorealistic fake identities of other people
- Stock-photo-style generic imagery with no identity lock
- Anyone who thinks filters and AI portraits are the same category (they are not the market Alux Art speaks to)

## 4. Brand Personality

Alux Art is:

| Trait | What it means in practice |
|---|---|
| **Precise** | Speaks with specificity. Never vague. "10 editorial images" not "lots of photos." |
| **Assured** | Does not hedge. Does not over-explain. The technology speaks through confidence in copy. |
| **Warm** | Not cold-tech. The product handles something personal — your face, your image. Tone acknowledges that. |
| **Efficient** | Respects the user's time. Short sentences. Short steps. Short waiting. |
| **Quietly luxurious** | Does not shout. Quality communicates itself. Like a premium hotel that doesn't scream about amenities — it shows them. |

Alux Art is **not**:
- Hype-driven ("stunning", "insane detail", "mind-blowing")
- Corporate-cold
- Overly technical (no AI jargon in user-facing copy)
- Apologetic or over-qualifying

## 5. Voice and Tone

### Core Voice
Direct. Clean. Assured. Warm enough to feel personal, not warm enough to feel casual.

### Sentence Shape
Short declarative sentences. One idea per sentence. No filler. No throat-clearing.

Preferred: "Upload your photos. We handle the rest."
Avoid: "With Alux Art's revolutionary AI technology, you can now experience a whole new world of photoshoot possibilities..."

### Active Voice Only
Never passive. The product does things. The user does things. Nobody vaguely happens to anything.

### Numbers Are Proof
Use specific numbers wherever possible. "10 images" not "a set of images." "2 minutes" not "a short wait." "₦15,000" not "affordable."

### Tone Calibration by Context

| Context | Tone register |
|---|---|
| Social media caption | Crisp, confident, 1–3 short sentences |
| Ad copy | Benefit-first, punchy, closes with a single action |
| Email subject line | Factual or curiosity — never clickbait |
| In-app UI | Minimal. Action-first. Verbs over nouns. |
| Brand story/about | Warm, personal, founder-voice |

### Forbidden Words in Copy
- stunning, beautiful, amazing, incredible, epic, revolutionary, game-changing
- just (diminishes the product: "just upload" — the product is not small)
- cutting-edge, state-of-the-art, next-generation (meaningless tech filler)
- content (when referring to photos — say "images" or "photos")
- leverage, utilize (corporate bloat)

## 6. Visual Identity

### Color Philosophy
The Alux Art palette is built around **teal**, **warm cream**, and **soft coral** — a combination that reads as modern, premium, and distinctly non-tech-bro. In dark mode it deepens to a rich teal-black that feels like a luxury studio environment.

The palette deliberately avoids the standard AI-product colors (neon purple, cyan-on-black, flat white-on-gray). It is warm without being soft, modern without being cold.

**Committed** — the teal family carries 40–60% of every surface. It is not a single accent; it is the identity. Coral is the counterpoint (10–15%). Sky blue is atmospheric (background gradients only).

### Imagery Style

**Generated output images** (the product itself):
- Editorial photography aesthetic
- Natural skin texture, realistic fabric folds, editorial lens feel
- Three-point studio lighting or location-specific setups
- Color grade: Kodak Vision3 / medium-format film aesthetic
- No heavy filters, no over-retouching, no AI "smoothness"

**Brand/marketing photography** (used in ads, social, website):
- Real people, diverse ages and skin tones
- Nigerian/West African context — urban settings, Lagos street texture, Abuja clean interiors
- Not stock photo. Not a Western ideal of "professional photo."
- Warm, natural light preferred over harsh studio flash in marketing imagery
- The product images speak for themselves — hero the output

**What to avoid in imagery**:
- Over-processed AI portrait look (plastic skin, unnatural sharpening)
- Generic stock diversity (five people of different ethnicities looking at a screen)
- Competitor-style neon/cyber aesthetics
- Posed corporate stiffness

### Logo Usage
- Always use on appropriate contrast (teal on cream/white, or white on teal/dark)
- Minimum clear space: equal to the height of the "A" in "Alux" on all sides
- Do not stretch, recolor, or add effects to the logo mark

### Dark / Light Modes
The product supports both. Dark mode is the premium default (studio-like, focused); light mode is clean and accessible. Both use the same teal-led palette — dark mode is not a gray inversion, it deepens toward teal-black.

| character base | Reference Base |
| [OUTFIT] tag | Outfit Reference |
| [HAIRSTYLE] tag | Hair Reference |
| [BACKGROUND] tag | Background Reference |
| inspiration image | Inspiration Photo |
| identity images | Your Photos |

### Pricing Language
- Never say "cheap" or "affordable" — say "₦15,000 for 10 professional images"
- Always anchor to what a real shoot costs: "vs. ₦80,000+ for a commercial shoot"
- USD pricing is ≈$10 per shoot — frame as accessible global pricing, not "discounted"

### Process Language (what happens when they shoot)
1. Upload your photos (identity)
2. Add an inspiration image (or reference images for Advanced)
3. We analyze your features and build a personalized brief
4. Your 10 images generate automatically
5. Download your gallery

Do not use: "AI generates," "the model produces," "our algorithms" — say "we" and let the brand carry the agency.

## 8. Brand Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- Lead with the output. Show the images. Let quality do the talking.
- Use "your likeness," "your face," "you in the image" — the identity lock is the core differentiator and should be named directly
- Speak specifically: ₦15,000, 10 images, 2–5 minutes
- Use the teal palette consistently and confidently
- Write in short sentences with high noun-to-adjective ratios
- Acknowledge the Nigerian context — pricing in naira, Paystack, familiar touchpoints

### Don't
- Use AI hype language ("revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "game-changing")
- Show competitor-comparison tables that center weaknesses
- Use the word "just" to minimize the product's value
- Show AI-generated people who don't match the uploaded identity (this undermines the core promise)
- Portray the product as a filter or beauty tool — it is a studio, not a beautifier
- Use passive voice or hedge language in CTAs
- Reference the underlying models (fal.ai, Gemini, Claude, Flux) in user-facing marketing — those are infrastructure, not features

## 9. Brand in Context

### What Alux Art is like
- **Sector feel**: premium creative services, not SaaS dashboard
- **Price point positioning**: accessible luxury — significantly cheaper than human photographers, positioned above filter apps and toy AI portrait apps
- **Trust signal**: identity fidelity is the headline feature. Customers are trusting the product with their face. The brand must earn that trust through precision in both the product and the language.

### Competitive Framing (internal, not for ads)
- vs. portrait photographer: faster, cheaper, on-demand, same output quality for headshots and editorials
- vs. AI portrait apps (Lensa, etc.): those replace your identity with a fantasy. Alux Art preserves your exact face.
- vs. photo editors / Lightroom: those edit existing photos. Alux Art generates new professional photos.

### Brand Archetype
The **Magician** with a **Sage** undertone. Transformative (turns ordinary photos into professional ones) without being showy about the technology behind it. The transformation is the point; the AI is the means, not the message.
